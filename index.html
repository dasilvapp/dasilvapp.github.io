Pedro Perfeito da Silva

I am a political economist interested in the challenges that financial globalization introduces for democratic regimes. As a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Department of Economics at the University of Leeds, I investigate the drivers and implications of financial nationalism in East-Central Europe and South America. My research agenda covers topics such as the political economy of neoliberalism and post-neoliberalism, the macroeconomic effects of financial liberalization, the role of State-owned banks, the impact of creative industries, and the policy preferences of populist parties.

My articles appeared in journals like Development and Change, Government and Opposition, Review of International Political Economy, and Third World Quarterly.
